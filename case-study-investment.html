Case Study 02

Financial Investment Onboarding

Simplified compliance-heavy investment onboarding from 12% to 84% completion by making regulatory requirements transparent, reducing form errors by 45%, and cutting abandonment time by 35%.

Executive Summary

Problem, Approach, Solution

Problem

Onboarding funnel dropped from 100 signups to 12% completion. Users abandoned during KYC steps, document uploads, and compliance questions. Forms had no inline validation, making errors compound.

Solution

Transparent progressive disclosure with conditional logic visible upfront. Inline validation, clear explanations for each regulatory question, save-and-resume, and visual progress tracking.

Impact

84% completion rate (+600%), 45% fewer form errors, 35% faster avg onboarding (15 min → 9.7 min). User trust scores improved from 62% to 81%.

User Research & Discovery

Understanding why users abandon the onboarding flow

Research Methods

Stakeholder Interviews

Conducted 8 interviews (45–60 min each) with compliance officers, investment advisors, and product managers. Mapped regulatory constraints and business rules.

Journey Analytics

Analyzed Mixpanel data tracking 2M+ onboarding attempts over 6 months. Identified drop-off points: 35% at KYC, 28% at document upload, 20% at accreditation questions.

Moderated Usability Testing

Recruited 12 first-time investors from target demographic. Ran task-based tests on existing flow. Observed confusion around conditional questions and regulatory language.

User Interviews

Conducted 5 post-abandonment interviews with users who started but didn't complete. Key insight: "I didn't understand why I was being asked those questions."

Key Findings

  • Regulatory surprise: Users didn't expect KYC/compliance steps, perceived them as bloat rather than necessary.
  • Hidden conditions: Conditional logic was backend-hidden; users didn't know upfront if they'd need extra docs.
  • Jargon barrier: Terms like "accreditation," "beneficial owner," and "source of funds" weren't explained.
  • Error frustration: No inline validation; users discovered errors only after submitting entire multi-step form.
  • Time uncertainty: No progress indication; users didn't know how many steps remained.

User Personas

Three distinct investment onboarding behaviors

Alex — First-Time Investor
Age 28, tech worker, moderate savings, minimal investment experience
Goal: Invest $5k quickly without overwhelming complexity or confusion about rules.

Needs: Clear explanation of each step, reassurance that it's safe, fast progress.
Frustration: Doesn't understand compliance jargon; abandon if it feels like a scam or too risky.

Jordan — High-Net-Worth Investor
Age 45, business owner, significant assets, works with advisors
Goal: Complete onboarding quickly while ensuring full compliance; may need special accreditation path.

Needs: Efficient process, clear accreditation rules, option to skip unnecessary steps.
Frustration: Generic one-size-fits-all flows waste time for complex situations.

Casey — Compliance Officer
Fintech internal stakeholder ensuring regulatory adherence
Goal: High completion rates with zero regulatory risk; audit-ready documentation.

Needs: Clear condition logic, audit trails, exception handling for edge cases.
Frustration: Balancing user experience with regulatory requirements is hard; don't want UX changes to hide compliance.

Information Architecture & User Flows

Transparent wireframing of conditional logic

Core User Flow

The flow branches based on investment amount and user type, but users see all branches upfront via conditional disclosure.

Standard User Path
Email Verify → Personal Info → Accreditation Check → Document Upload → Review Rules → Link Bank → Deposit → Invest → Confirmation
Accredited Investor Path
Email Verify → Personal Info → Income Verification → Accreditation Fast-Track → Review Rules → Link Bank → Fund → Invest
Corporate Entity Path
Email Verify → Entity Info → Beneficial Owner Form → W-9 Upload → Review Rules → Link Bank → Fund → Invest

Progressive Disclosure Strategy

Information hierarchy: Show only what's relevant. Upfront list shows all potential steps; visible state toggles based on conditions.

Step Type Always Shown Conditionally Shown
Account Verification Email confirmation Phone verification (if flagged)
Identity Verification Name, DOB, SSN Beneficial owner (if entity), Previous address (if flagged)
Investment Eligibility Accreditation checkbox Income upload (if selected), Tax forms (if needed)
Banking Bank account linking Wire instructions (if international)

Key Design Decisions

Strategic wireframing and interaction patterns

1

Upfront Condition Visibility

Rather than hiding conditional steps, we show users an upfront checklist of "What You'll Need" with icons indicating which steps apply to them. This eliminates surprise complexity.

2

Contextual Help for Regulatory Terms

Every compliance question has an inline help tooltip explaining the "why" in plain language. Example: "We ask about accreditation to ensure you understand investment risk."

3

Real-Time Inline Validation

Form fields validate as users type (e.g., SSN format, email domain). Success/error states appear immediately with actionable guidance. No form submission surprises.

4

Save & Resume Capability

Users can pause mid-flow and resume from their last saved state. Auto-save occurs every 30 seconds. This removes pressure and accommodates users gathering documents.

5

Visual Progress & Effort Estimation

Persistent progress bar at top shows "Step 2 of 8 (5 min remaining)" dynamically. Users understand exactly how much work remains.

6

Trust Signals & Compliance Transparency

Badges highlight regulatory compliance ("SEC Registered," "FINRA Member"). Security icons and encryption messaging reduce fraud concerns.

Accessibility & Inclusive Design

WCAG 2.1 Level AA compliance for financial services

Research Insight: Financial onboarding users span ages 18–75 with varying tech comfort. Accessibility features like clear focus states and plain language benefit all users, not just those with disabilities.

Accessible Patterns

Feature Implementation Benefit
Logical Tab Order Form fields follow reading order. Skip link jumps to form. Tabindex managed explicitly. Keyboard-only users navigate efficiently without jumping around
Error Messages Errors associated with form fields via aria-describedby. Screen readers announce inline. Users with screen readers understand errors immediately, not after form submission
Label Association <label for="inputId"> for all form inputs. Clear, visible labels always. Screen reader users know what each field is for
Color Independence Status indicators use icons + text, not color alone. Contrast 4.5:1+ on all text. Users with color blindness or low vision can interpret status
Focus Visibility 3px blue outline with 2px offset on all interactive elements. Never removed. Users with tremor or low vision always know where they are

Responsive Design Approach

Desktop, tablet, and mobile-optimized flows

Financial onboarding requires secure data entry. Design prioritizes single-column layouts on mobile with large touch targets (48px minimum) and clear visual hierarchy.

Old Flow (Desktop)

Multi-step modal or page transitions. Users could lose context between steps. Progress indicator unclear. No intermediate save.

New Flow (Responsive)

Single-column layout on all devices. Persistent progress bar. Full-width inputs (48px+ height). Touch-friendly upload buttons. Works flawlessly on mobile.

Testing & Iteration

Validating the design through user feedback loops

Usability Testing Sessions

Round 1: Wireframes

4 first-time investors reviewed wireframes. Feedback: "I still don't understand why I need to upload a W-9" → Led to contextual help tooltips.

Round 2: Prototypes

6 users tested interactive prototype. Measured: avg 11 min to complete. Error rate 18%. Key insight: Progressive disclosure reduced perceived complexity.

Round 3: Live MVP

Soft-launched with 5% of users. Monitored funnel, errors, and support tickets. Measured 67% completion vs. 12% baseline. User satisfaction: 4.2/5.

Compliance Audit

Legal + compliance team reviewed wireframes and prototype. Verified all regulatory requirements were met, audit trails logged, and conditional logic correct.

Iterative Changes

  • Iteration 1: Added "What You'll Need" checklist upfront after users said the form felt overwhelming.
  • Iteration 2: Replaced modal errors with inline field-level feedback to prevent form abandonment.
  • Iteration 3: Introduced save-and-resume after observing users leaving to gather documents.
  • Iteration 4: Added trust badges (SEC/FINRA) and encryption icons after user interviews revealed security concerns.

Key Learnings & Takeaways

Insights for fintech and regulated onboarding experiences

1. Transparency Over Compliance Obfuscation: Upfront visibility of all steps and conditions paradoxically increased completion. Users felt more in control, not less.
2. Plain Language Is a Regulatory Asset: Explaining why compliance questions matter in user terms reduced abandonment and improved audit readiness.
3. Inline Validation Prevents Compound Errors: Real-time feedback eliminated end-of-flow error pileups and frustration-driven abandonment.
4. Save-and-Resume Removes Pressure: Users willing to abandon mid-flow if they can resume. This removed the urgency penalty for complex requirements.
5. Visual Progress Improves Trust: Time/step indicators gave users confidence; unclear progress led to abandonment fears.
6. Accessibility Improves Usability for All: Features designed for screen reader or keyboard users (clear labels, logical order) benefited all users, especially on mobile.
