Research & Design Process

Our approach follows a structured 5-phase process that places user research at the center of every decision.

1. Discover

Goal: Understand users, their context, and their needs.

Methods: Contextual inquiry, interviews, surveys, workflow mapping, competitive analysis.

Outcome: Research findings, user personas, empathy maps, problem statements.

2–3 weeks
2. Define

Goal: Synthesize insights into clear, actionable problem definitions.

Methods: Affinity mapping, journey mapping, user flows, opportunity matrices, prioritization.

Outcome: Design brief, user flows, information architecture, requirements matrix.

1–2 weeks
3. Design

Goal: Create solutions that address user needs and business goals.

Methods: Wireframing, prototyping, design systems, interaction design, visual design.

Outcome: Low-fidelity wireframes, high-fidelity prototypes, design specifications.

3–4 weeks
4. Test & Validate

Goal: Verify solutions work for real users in real contexts.

Methods: Usability testing, A/B testing, accessibility audits, analytics review, feedback loops.

Outcome: Test report, validated design decisions, iteration roadmap.

2–3 weeks
5. Iterate & Learn

Goal: Continuously improve based on user behavior and feedback.

Methods: Post-launch analytics, user interviews, support feedback, performance metrics.

Outcome: Iteration priorities, performance baseline, learned patterns for future work.

Ongoing

Research Methods & Techniques

🔍

Contextual Inquiry

Observe users in their natural environment performing real tasks. Capture workflow patterns, pain points, workarounds, and unspoken needs that interviews might miss.

Best for: Understanding complex workflows, domain expertise gaps, environmental context

💬

In-Depth Interviews

Semi-structured conversations to explore user needs, motivations, frustrations, and mental models. Allows for follow-up questions and deeper understanding.

Best for: Exploring motivations, understanding decision-making, discovering edge cases

📋

Surveys & Questionnaires

Structured data collection from larger user populations. Quantifies preferences, behaviors, and satisfaction. Complements qualitative research.

Best for: Validation, large-scale patterns, demographic insights

🗺️

User Journey Mapping

Visualize the end-to-end user experience across touchpoints, channels, and time. Identify moments of truth, pain points, and opportunities for improvement.

Best for: Holistic understanding, cross-channel optimization, stakeholder alignment

🎯

Usability Testing

Have users attempt defined tasks with prototypes or live systems. Measure success rates, time-on-task, error rates, and gather feedback on usability.

Best for: Validating prototypes, identifying friction, prioritizing iterations

📊

Analytics & Data Analysis

Analyze user behavior data: click patterns, conversion rates, drop-off points, feature usage. Uncover patterns that self-reported data might miss.

Best for: Validating hypotheses, discovering unexpected patterns, measuring impact

User flows visualize the steps users take to complete a task. They reveal decision points, alternate paths, and opportunities for optimization.

Key Research Insights Framework

Insight Type Questions to Ask Output / Action
Needs Insights What do users need? What problems are they solving? Prioritized needs list, user stories, requirements
Behavior Insights How do users currently work? What are their workflows? Journey maps, user flows, context of use
Mental Model Insights How do users think about the problem? What's their language? IA structure, terminology, conceptual models
Motivation Insights Why do users care? What are their goals and constraints? Prioritization, messaging, feature decisions
Friction Insights Where do users struggle? What causes errors or delays? Interaction design improvements, safeguards

Ensuring Research Quality

✓

Sample Diversity

Recruit users across different experience levels, demographics, and use contexts. Avoid skewing toward "power users" or biasing toward one persona.

✓

Triangulation

Use multiple research methods to validate findings. If interviews, observations, and analytics all point to the same pattern, confidence is high.

✓

Bias Awareness

Be aware of confirmation bias (only seeing what we expect), selection bias (recruiting the "wrong" users), and observer bias (affecting user behavior).

✓

Iteration & Feedback Loops

Research is never "done." Continuous feedback from users throughout design and post-launch ensures decisions remain grounded in user reality.

✓

Documentation & Sharing

Document research findings clearly and share with stakeholders. Makes research insights visible and prevents them from being lost or misinterpreted.

AI-Assisted UX Research

Leveraging AI tools to scale research, analyze data faster, and identify patterns that might be missed in manual analysis.

Automated Interview Synthesis

AI transcribes interviews and identifies key themes, quotes, and patterns. Researchers review and validate, then create synthesis docs 3x faster.

Survey Analysis

Natural language processing analyzes open-ended survey responses. Surfaces sentiment, themes, and outliers without manual coding.

Behavioral Pattern Detection

ML models detect patterns in user analytics that humans might miss. Flags anomalies, predicts churn, segments users by behavior.

Competitive Intelligence

AI scrapes and analyzes competitor features, pricing, messaging. Tracks market changes and identifies emerging patterns.

Persona Generation

AI synthesizes research data into persona profiles, jobs to be done, and pain point summaries. Provides starting point for team refinement.

Research Documentation

Auto-generates research reports, slide decks, and summaries from raw data. Saves time on documentation overhead.
